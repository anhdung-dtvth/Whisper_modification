Technical Research Review

WhisperSign: Spatio-Temporal Transformer for Continuous Sign Language Recognition

Nguyen Minh Duc
Department of Physics

Abstract

This report presents WhisperSign, a novel architecture adapted from the Whisper transformer for the task of continuous sign language recognition (SLR). Unlike traditional computer vision approaches that utilize raw video pixels, WhisperSign operates on low-dimensional 3D skeletal sequences. We propose a three-stage training pipeline and a hybrid CTC-Attention loss function to bridge the gap between skeletal motion and linguistic representation. Furthermore, we demonstrate real-time inference capabilities using Leap Motion sensors, achieving low-latency recognition suitable for interactive applications.

1. Introduction

Sign language recognition (SLR) remains a critical challenge at the intersection of computer vision, linguistics, and human-computer interaction. While recent advances in Large Language Models (LLMs) have revolutionized text processing, sign language requires modeling complex spatio-temporal dependencies in 3D space.

WhisperSign is designed to leverage the powerful temporal modeling capabilities of the Transformer architecture while replacing the traditional acoustic frontend with a specialized Sign Language Frontend. This allows the model to process 3D joint positions and velocities, effectively treating sign language as a "visual speech" signal.

2. Methodology

2.1 Input Representation & Normalization

The model accepts inputs as tensors of shape (B, T, 42, 7). Before being processed by the transformer, the signal undergoes a two-step normalization process to achieve spatial and scale invariance:

  • Palm-Centric Spatial Normalization: Coordinates are transformed relative to the palm index. For left hand (0-20), the wrist (index 0) is the origin. For right hand (21-41), the wrist (index 21) is the origin.
    P̂ᵢ = Pᵢ - P_palm
  • Bone-Chain Scale Normalization: To normalize across users with different hand sizes, a reference bone chain length L_ref is computed using the middle finger chain: Wrist(0)→MCP(9)→PIP(10)→DIP(11)→TIP(12).
    P̂ᵢ = Pᵢ / L_ref, where L_ref is the average middle finger bone chain length across the clip.

Feature Vector (F=7)

Each joint contains [x, y, z, vx, vy, vz, conf]. Velocities (vx, vy, vz) are also scaled by L_ref to maintain physical consistency.

Augmentation Strategy

To simulate infrared sensor noise and occlusion:

  • Joint Masking: 15% probability of zeroing a joint.
  • Temporal Masking: 10% prob. (max 10 frames).
  • Noise: Gaussian σ=0.005.

2.2 Model Architecture

graph TD A[Skeletal Sequence T, 42, 7] --> B[Spatio-Temporal Frontend] B --> C[Transformer Encoder 6 Layers] C --> D[Hybrid Decoder] D --> E[CTC Head Output] D --> F[Attention Decoder Output] E & F --> G[Joint Inference Logic]

The architecture consists of three primary modules:

  • SignLanguageFrontend: Projects joint data into a latent space using linear layers followed by spatial dropout and temporal patch grouping.
  • SpatioTemporalEncoder: A 6-layer Transformer encoder modeled after the Whisper-Base architecture, capturing long-range temporal dependencies.
  • TwoPassDecoder: A hybrid system that uses Connectionist Temporal Classification (CTC) for efficient continuous decoding and an Attention-based decoder for linguistic refinement.

3. Multi-Stage Training & Hybrid Loss

WhisperSign utilizes a dual-objective training strategy to optimize both sequence alignment and linguistic structure. The total loss L is defined as:

L = α · L_CTC + (1 - α) · L_Att
where L_CTC is the Connectionist Temporal Classification loss and L_Att is the Cross-Entropy loss from the autoregressive Attention decoder.

Stage 1

Frontend Warm-up (α=1.0)

Encoder and Decoder are frozen. The model focuses exclusively on learning robust skeletal embeddings via CTC loss.

Stage 2

Joint Training (α=0.3)

All layers are unfrozen. The Attention decoder rescents CTC hypotheses, forcing the model to learn linguistic dependencies.

Stage 3

Inference Tuning

Focuses on temporal jitter robustness and sliding window consistency for real-time deployment.

4. Robustness & Sensor Fusion

4.1 Automated Skeleton Repair & Feature Engineering

A significant challenge in deployment is the variety of sensor outputs (MediaPipe, Holistic, Kinect, Leap Motion). WhisperSign implements a Robust Self-Repair logic within the SignLanguageDataset class that dynamically detects input dimensionality and applies deterministic slicing:

  • Dual-Hand Standard: (T, 42, 7) format using MediaPipe Hands convention.
  • Pose + Hands Fusion: (T, 201) and (T, 225) shapes are automatically reshaped to (T, J_total, 3) and sliced to extract the 42 hand-centric joints, discarding redundant body pose landmarks.
  • Holistic Mapping: (T, 543) joint inputs are mapped to the 501-542 index range to extract precise hand articulation data.

This repair layer ensures that the SpatioTemporalEncoder always receives a consistent 42-joint input, regardless of whether the data was processed locally or in the cloud.

4.2 Real-Time Leap Motion Integration

The project now includes high-frequency (60-120Hz) Leap Motion support via scripts/leap_realtime_inference.py. This bridge script utilizes the LeapMotionAdapter to transform absolute millimeter coordinates into the model's expected hand-centric normalized space.
